This website was created with the intention of educating people about different learning disabilities. As the cliche goes "Before you judge a man, walk a mile in his shoes". Replace "man" and "his" with "student" and "his or her" respectively and you get the gist. Realistically, we can never truly understand what it is like to experience a learning disability if we do not have one personally, in part because each individual's experience is different. However, we can gain a greater understanding of the emotions surrounding attempting to learn with a cognitive deficit. When you click on the arrow below you will see three floating bubbles, each containing a simulation of a different learning disability. While these simulations cannot claim to let you see what it's like to write with dysgraphia or do math with dyscalculia, they can begin to mimic the struggle that students with these disabilities experience constantly. Hopefully, these simulations, coupled with the educator tips following them can not only encourage you feel empathy for these students, but know how to help them succeed as well.
